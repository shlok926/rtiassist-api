## 🏛️ What is RTI? — Full Overview

### Definition

**The Right to Information (RTI)** is a fundamental right of every Indian citizen guaranteed under **Article 19(1)(a)** of the Constitution of India, read with **The Right to Information Act, 2005**. It empowers citizens to **seek information from any government or public authority**, promoting transparency, accountability, and the fight against corruption.

Under this right, any Indian citizen can formally request information from:
- Central Government ministries and departments
- State Government offices
- Local bodies (Municipal Corporations, Gram Panchayats, Panchayat Raj institutions)
- Public sector undertakings (PSUs)
- Courts, regulatory bodies, and constitutional authorities
- NGOs substantially funded by the government

---

### Historical Background

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1975 | Supreme Court in *State of UP v. Raj Narain* held that citizens have the right to know |
| 1996 | Mazdoor Kisan Shakti Sangathan (MKSS) pioneered mass RTI campaigns in Rajasthan |
| 2002 | Freedom of Information Act passed (but never notified) |
| **2005** | **Right to Information Act, 2005 enacted — came into force on 12 October 2005** |
| 2019 | RTI (Amendment) Act — changed service tenures of CIC/SIC commissioners |

---

### What Information Can You Request?

Under RTI, a citizen can request:
- ✅ Records, documents, memos, emails, opinions, advice, press releases
- ✅ Contracts, reports, papers, samples, data held in any electronic form
- ✅ Certified copies of any document held by the public authority
- ✅ Inspection of works in progress, records, and government documents
- ✅ Samples of material used in government construction / procurement

---

### What Information is EXEMPT? (Section 8)

| What | Why Exempt |
|------|-----------|
| National security, sovereignty, intelligence | Protects India's strategic interests |
| Court-prohibited information | Judicial confidentiality |
| Commercial secrets / IP | Competitive / economic harm |
| Fiduciary relationships | Trust-based confidentiality |
| Personal private information | Privacy protection |
| Cabinet papers (before decision) | Deliberative process protection |

> **Key citizen right:** Even exempt information **can be disclosed** if public interest in disclosure outweighs the harm — under Section 8(2). Most citizens and even PIOs miss this.

### How to File an RTI Application — Step by Step

```
Step 1 ── Identify the PIO (Public Information Officer) of the concerned department

Step 2 ── Write your application in plain language
           No specific format required by law

Step 3 ── Pay the fee
           ₹10 for Central Govt | Varies by state (₹10–₹50)
           BPL cardholders: COMPLETELY FREE

Step 4 ── Submit
           By post / in-person / online at https://rtionline.gov.in

Step 5 ── Wait for response — within 30 days
           (48 hours if life/liberty at stake)

Step 6 ── No response or unsatisfied?
           → File First Appeal to First Appellate Authority (within 30 days)

Step 7 ── Still unsatisfied?
           → File Second Appeal to CIC/SIC (within 90 days)
```

## 📜 RTI Act 2005 — Key Rules & Sections

### Core Sections Every Citizen Must Know

| Section | Title | What It Means for You |
|---------|-------|-----------------------|
| **Sec 2(f)** | Definition of "Information" | Anything held in any form by a public authority — documents, records, emails, data |
| **Sec 2(h)** | Definition of "Public Authority" | Any body owned/controlled/financed by government, including NGOs substantially funded |
| **Sec 3** | Right to Information | Every citizen of India has this right — no educational qualification required |
| **Sec 4** | Proactive Disclosure (Suo Motu) | Every public authority MUST publish key information without you asking |
| **Sec 5** | Designation of PIOs | Every public authority must designate a Public Information Officer |
| **Sec 6** | Filing the Application | In writing or electronic; **no reasons required** to be given for seeking information |
| **Sec 7** | Disposal of Request | Response must come within **30 days**; **48 hours** if life/liberty is at stake |
| **Sec 8** | Exemptions | 10 categories of exempt information (national security, privacy, etc.) |
| **Sec 9** | Grounds of Rejection | PIO can reject only on specific legal grounds — must state the reason in writing |
| **Sec 11** | Third Party Information | If info involves third party, PIO must notify them; timeline extends to 40 days |
| **Sec 18** | Powers of Information Commission | CIC/SIC can examine records, impose penalties, award compensation to applicant |
| **Sec 19** | Appeals | **First Appeal** → FAA within 30 days; **Second Appeal** → CIC/SIC within 90 days |
| **Sec 20** | Penalties | PIO fined **₹250/day** (max ₹25,000) + disciplinary action for malicious denial |
| **Sec 22** | RTI Overrides Other Laws | RTI Act overrides the Official Secrets Act, 1923 — a major citizen protection |
| **Sec 23** | Bar of Courts | Civil courts cannot intervene in RTI matters — only Information Commissions can |

---

### Key RTI Rules Under Central Information Commission Rules, 2007

- **Rule 3:** Application must be in writing/electronic — English, Hindi, or official local language
- **Rule 4:** First Appeal fee ₹10; **no fee** for Second Appeal to CIC
- **Rule 5:** PIO must provide acknowledgment / receipt of your request
- **Rule 7:** PIO **cannot ask you for the reason** behind your information request
- **Rule 8:** Information concerning life or liberty must be provided within **48 hours**

---

### RTI Response & Appeal Timeline

```
Day 0     ── You file RTI application
              │
Day 1–30  ── Normal response window
              │  ├── Life/liberty: 48 HOURS mandatory
              │  └── Third party involved: 40 days
              │
Day 30    ── No response = Deemed Refusal → File FIRST APPEAL (within 30 days)
              │
Day 31–60 ── First Appellate Authority (FAA) must decide within 30 days
              │  (max 45 days if stated in writing)
              │
Day 90+   ── Second Appeal to CIC (Central) or SIC (State)
              └── PIO penalisable ₹250/day (max ₹25,000) + disciplinary action
```

## ⚠️ Disclaimer & Important RTI Rules

RTIAssist is an informational and productivity tool. It does **not** constitute legal advice.

### Important Rules Before You File

1. **You do NOT need to give reasons** for your RTI request under Section 6 — never explain why you want the information
2. **BPL cardholders file for FREE** — carry a copy of your BPL card when filing in person
3. **Verify the PIO name and address** before filing — officers change; use the department's official website
4. **Review the generated draft** before submitting — AI-generated content should always be reviewed
5. **30-day deadline is strict** — file your First Appeal exactly 30 days from the date of filing, not from when you expected a response
6. **Section 8 exemptions are not absolute** — even "exempt" information can be disclosed if public interest justifies it under Section 8(2)
7. **RTIAssist stores no data on servers** — all tracker data is stored locally in your browser's localStorage only
8. For complex legal matters or First/Second Appeal hearings, consult a qualified RTI activist, paralegal, or advocate
